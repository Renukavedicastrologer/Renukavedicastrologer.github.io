The 27 Nakshatras: Lunar Mansions of the Sky

May 2025

In Vedic astrology, the sky is divided not just into the familiar 12 signs of the zodiac, but into 27 (sometimes 28) Nakshatras — lunar mansions through which the Moon travels during its monthly cycle. These star clusters form the oldest layer of astrological knowledge in Jyotish, predating even the planetary system used today.

"The Nakshatras are the abodes of the gods — each one a window into a particular quality of cosmic energy, a specific vibration of Shakti made manifest in the sky."

What is a Nakshatra?

The word Nakshatra derives from Sanskrit: naksha (to approach, to attain) and tra (a vehicle or instrument). Together, the Nakshatras are vehicles through which the Moon's energy reaches the Earth — coloring every moment with a distinct psychological and spiritual quality.

The 360-degree zodiac is divided into 27 equal parts of 13°20' each. The Moon, moving approximately 13 degrees per day, transits one Nakshatra per day, completing the full circuit in roughly 27 days — the sidereal lunar month.

The Three Groups: Deva, Nara, and Rakshasa

The Nakshatras are traditionally classified into three temperamental groups:

  • Deva Nakshatras — Divine, sattvic in nature; gentle, giving, spiritually inclined
  • Nara Nakshatras — Human, rajasic; balanced between spiritual and material
  • Rakshasa Nakshatras — Demoniac, tamasic; intense, powerful, transformative

Key Nakshatras to Know

Ashwini (0°–13°20' Aries)

The first Nakshatra, ruled by the twin divine physicians, the Ashwins. Associated with swift healing, new beginnings, and the impulse to act. Those born with Moon in Ashwini are often quick, enterprising, and drawn to medicine or healing arts.

Rohini (10°–23°20' Taurus)

The Moon's own favourite Nakshatra — the "Red One," associated with Brahma as creator. Rohini is lush, fertile, and creative, carrying energies of abundance, beauty, and sensory pleasure. It is the Nakshatra of artists, gardeners, and lovers of the beautiful.

Ardra (6°40'–20° Gemini)

Ruled by Rudra, the storm god. Ardra brings intensity, emotional depth, and the power of dissolution before renewal. Its symbol is a teardrop — signifying both grief and release. Powerfully placed in the charts of those who undergo profound transformation.

Using Nakshatras in Practice

The Nakshatra of your natal Moon is one of the most important factors in a Vedic chart. It determines your Janma Nakshatra — the birth star — which forms the basis of your Dasha sequence in the Vimshottari system, your compatibility analysis in Kundali matching, and your most intimate psychological nature.

Learning to read Nakshatras adds extraordinary depth to chart interpretation, revealing layers of meaning invisible to the Western astrological eye.
